Top Three Bar Picks

Russia is know for its wild party scene. If you want to get as much out of your nights as your days, I recommend scheduling your days so you can be back at the hostel for around 6 p.m. Take a 2 -3 hour nap, wake up, take a shower, grab a bite to eat, and go out around 11.

There are countless bars in Moscow, and we visited far to many to recount on this website. Here are our top three pics:

Karma Bar

A chic club that is half hookah-lounge, half dance floor. Avant garde artwork and black and white photos add to the decor. Known to be a despot bar, it is the only bar we visited that played popular western music.

This bar is pricey - splurge here your last night out, or pre-game at one of the many small bars nearby. Chinese Pilot down the road sells cheap bear and features a regular jazz line-up. Karma Bar is also a known for hosting jazz musicians (pictured above).

Note: Jazz music is popular in Moscow; however, the jazz I heard was much different than the jazz heard drifting through the streets of my hometown, New Orleans. Russian jazz has a heavy industrial-music influence, spiked with brooding, distorted guitars, atmospheric keyboards and heavy use of symbols. The sound has more in common with a band like Nine Inch Nails than anything on Bourbon St.

The Hungry Duck

The legendary wildest bar in Europe. In the interest of keeping this discussion suitable for all ages - and not tarnishing my magnanimous character - my review of the Duck is heavily abridged.

The Hungry Duck's wikipedia page provides a suitable foundation for its boastful reputation. The page claims the Duck is tamer then it used to be - an assessment that honestly frightens me more than it intrigues.

You can also visit their tasteful (sarcasm) and family-values conscious (not recommended viewing for anyone under 18) website.

There was a cover charge, which if I remember was inexpensive. The Duck only serves beer: a domestic brand and Miller Genuine Draft (popular in Russia . . . strange, for a country with such expensive taste).

Propaganda

Fun dance club with cheap prices. If you like club music with a taste of reggae dubbed in, you'll have a good time here.

Come early for dinner, or visit for lunch if you are in the neighborhood. The food is delicious, and under almost everything is under 10 dollars.

It's hard to order at the bar from a menu you can't read, so unless you know a little Russian you are pretty much limited to Miller Genuine Draft (ugh!) or rum and coke (phonetically spoken the same in Russian).

A popular cocktail at the time we were there was a drink called Burn, a mixture of Red Bull and Absinthe. Inexpensive and delicious (if you like licorish).

Be prepared to get treated well at the bar . . . it is known that Americans tip higher then Russians (who tip 10 percent max, if at all).

Just for fun . . . Billionaire

Located in the Zamoskvorechie district, this exclusive nightclub is rumored to request a copy of your last pay check at the door: your payment must be at least six-figures to enter.
